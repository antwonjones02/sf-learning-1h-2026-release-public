Act on these

Two things need a plan now

Both have firm dates. Miss them and things stop working.

November 20, 2026 Hard cutoff

The old way our systems talk to each other gets shut off

What it is
An older “handshake” called the Token Server. It lets outside tools connect to our learning system.
What you will see
Nothing, until something breaks. After November 20, any tool still using the old handshake will stop working.
What needs to happen
Our IT and integration teams switch every connection to the new method (called OAuth 2.0). It is more secure and the modern standard.
Who to ask
The team that manages our SAP integrations and any vendors who connect to our learning data.
November 2026 Hard cutoff

Webex Training Center stops working for live virtual classes

What it is
One of the tools we can use to run live virtual training. SAP is dropping support for it.
What you will see
If you teach or attend a live class through Webex Training Center, it will stop launching from our learning system.
What needs to happen
Move those classes to Webex Meetings, Zoom, or Microsoft Teams, all are still supported.
Who to ask
The Learning team and instructors who run live virtual classes.
Two more deadlines further out (May 2027): the old learning search box and a free-of-charge subscription option both go away. Less urgent, but worth noting on the planning calendar.
What changes for people

Here is what your people will actually see

No tech talk. Just the visible, day-to-day differences for each group.

Some changes affect more than one group, so the totals overlap.

👨🏻‍💻

Employees

17 changes
  • Search inside their own learning. A search box now sits on the “View All” page so they can type a word and find an assignment instead of scrolling.
  • See course details right on the home page. Click a course card and details show up there, no extra page jump.
  • Reset their own e-signature PIN. A “Forgot My PIN” link appears on the home page. No more help-desk ticket.
  • New look for Programs and Curricula pages. Cleaner design that makes progress easier to track.
  • Clearer view of retraining. When a course needs to be retaken, the home page makes it obvious so deadlines stop getting missed.
  • Mobile gets better: they can see compliance status, find observers for tasks, and (for regulated teams) use the new Validated Learning app on Android.
  • Mobile loses a banner. The “Try the new experience” banner disappears, the new experience is now just the experience.
  • Small visual polish across the platform, cleaner buttons, tighter spacing.
👨🏻‍💼

Managers

4 changes
  • Take action on their team in one place. The Team View page now lets them register people for classes, withdraw them, pull reports, and handle SF-182 requests, without bouncing to other screens.
  • Switch roles with tabs. Managers who also act as Alternate Manager or HR Business Partner pick a tab to switch, no more menu hunting.
  • Lock task validation to the right person. If your compliance rules require the direct manager to sign off, you can now enforce that.
  • Faster, more reliable program completion reports, no more timeouts on big programs.
  • (Mobile) See team compliance at a glance. Curricula status now shows up in the mobile app for managers.
🛡️

Administrators

23 changes
  • One unified page for managing super-admin access (the old tool got split into two clearer pieces).
  • Bulk-edit course duration across many items at once instead of one at a time.
  • Auto-sort third-party content into a default library when it lands.
  • Capture more details on external learning (events outside the LMS).
  • Stronger privacy when copying production data into test environments, PII for external learners can be scrubbed automatically.
  • AI-suggested skills tagging for third-party content is now generally available (no longer a preview).
  • Better integration tools for organizations whose employees hold more than one role.
  • Plus 12 smaller polish items, see the full list for details.
Why it matters

The business story in five threads

⏱️

Time back for everyone

Dozens of small changes (better search, fewer clicks, bulk edits, in-page details) add up to less time spent finding learning and more time spent doing it. Frontline workers and admins benefit most.

📱

Mobile is now first-class

Five major mobile updates land this release. Field, frontline, and shift workers can complete training, track compliance, and assign observers from their phones, not just executives at desks.

✅

Compliance gets sharper

Better retraining visibility, manager-only sign-off on regulated tasks, and stronger PII protection in test environments together reduce audit risk and rework.

🤖

AI starts to scale

AI-suggested skill tags and AI-translated content reduce manual work for our learning team. AI Opportunities (skill ratings, more languages) are available if we choose to invest.

🔒

Two risks get retired, if we move

Two old systems shut off this November. Acting now means zero disruption. Acting late means broken integrations and broken virtual classrooms.

Good news

What you do not need to worry about

  • No downtime is expected. SAP rolls out the update over a weekend.
  • Most changes happen automatically. 25 of the 30 updates need no setup from us.
  • Our existing customizations keep working. Older settings and reports still run.
  • AI Opportunities are opt-in only. Nothing turns on without a conversation with SAP first.
Talk to your team

Three questions worth asking

  1. 1

    “Are we still using the old Token Server anywhere?”

    Ask IT or our SAP integrations team. If yes, we need a migration plan to OAuth 2.0 before November 20, 2026.

  2. 2

    “Do any of our live virtual classes still run on Webex Training Center?”

    Ask the Learning team. If yes, we need to move them to Webex Meetings, Zoom, or Teams before November 2026.

  3. 3

    “Should we explore the new AI Opportunities?”

    The AI Opportunities are powerful but require additional licensing, they need a separate conversation with our SAP rep about cost, scope, and rollout.
